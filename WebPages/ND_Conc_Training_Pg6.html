Student-Athlete Responsibilities
It is your responsibility to protect yourself, your teammates, and other players by playing responsibly and following concussion guidelines. Click on the buttons to review a summary of your primary responsibilities.
Education and Awareness
  • Know the signs and symptoms of concussions.
  • Complete all required concussion training, including any required trainings specific to your sport.
  • Acknowledge your responsibility for self-reporting and following concussion guidelines.
Recognition and Reporting
  • Remain alert for and immediately report any symptoms of a concussion that you experience.
  • If you have any reason to believe that you may have experienced a concussion, such as a blow to the head, immediately remove yourself from play and request evaluation.
  • Remain alert for symptoms in teammates and other players and report accordingly.
Cooperating with Evaluations
  • Complete all required baseline assessments as directed.
  • When being evaluated, you must be honest; never attempt to hide symptoms or misdirect coaches or medical staff.
  • Never attempt to return to play before receiving full and adequate evaluation.
Following Medical Guidance
  • If referred for medical treatment, you must receive treatment as directed.
  • Follow all medical advice and requirements for rest and recovery.
  • In cases where monitoring is required, remain with your assigned monitor and keep him or her informed of your condition.
  • Notify your team physician or doctor if symptoms worsen or new symptoms arise.
Adhering to Return to Play Protocols
  • Never attempt to return to play on the same day.
  • Comply with all restrictions on activities under return to play guidelines as directed by your coaching and medical staff.
  • Do not return to play in any capacity until clearance is received from your team physician and only your team physician.
