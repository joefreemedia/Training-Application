Return to Play
The protocol for return to play is a five-stage process, in which a student-athlete's progression is carefully monitored and he or she is gradually returned to full activity.
Return to Play
Stage Functional Excercise Stage Objective
1. No Activity Complete physical and cognitive rest Recovery
2. Light Aerobic Exercise Non-impact, light aerobic exercise with limits on movement and heart rate intensity Increase heart rate
3. Sport-Specific Exercise Non-contact, simple movement patterns specific to sport Add movement
4. Non-Contact Training Drills Progression to more complex training drills Exercise, coordination, and cognitive load
5. Full-Contact Practice Following medical clearance, participate in normal activities Restore confidence and assess functional skills
6. Return to Play Normal game play Win
If symptoms arise during stage-to-stage progression, the student-athlete
will return to earlier asymptomatic stage.
